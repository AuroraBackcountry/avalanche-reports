Key Actions & Safety Considerations

\n
  • Monitor natural avalanche releases actively, particularly in the Shale chute area.
  • Use conservative route selection, especially above the 1600m snowline where hazards are accentuated.
  • Maintain vigilance as the moderate hazard in the alpine zone may increase with the upcoming warm atmospheric river event.
  • Follow groomed routes where conditions are track-packed and stable, but reassess if new releases are observed.
  • Regularly check updated forecasts and be prepared to adjust plans based on evolving stability conditions.

Closing Summary

\n
This report outlines observed natural size 2 storm slab avalanches with moderate hazard ratings in the alpine zone, primarily driven by recent precipitation and the influence of a warm atmospheric river event. Cautious travel with an emphasis on conservative decision-making remains paramount as conditions may evolve rapidly. Operators and guides should prioritize safe, well-assessed routes above 1600m and re-assess terrain as weather conditions change. Stay updated and be prepared for potential increases in instability, ensuring that all backcountry trips are planned with current hazard awareness in mind.
