Closing Summary

The current conditions at Lake Louise present a considerable avalanche hazard in alpine terrain with moderate hazards at treeline and low hazards below. Although new storm slabs and dry loose avalanches have been observed, the overall snowpack maintains moderately hard facets and unreactive weak layers. Cautious route selection, an awareness of wind-affected zones, and avoidance of steep, rapidly loaded slopes are advised to manage the risks effectively. Seek out the best quality snow with stable support and remain vigilant for any shifts in weather or snowpack conditions.
