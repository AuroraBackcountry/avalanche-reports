Snowpack Description

Surface snow is moist to wet below 1800m, drying to moist and then dry above 2050m. New storm and wind slabs (10–30 cm thick) are developing above mid to upper tree line on or near supportive melt-freeze crust layers. A widespread, supportive mid-November MFcr exists 20–40 cm down, with snow moist beneath it at lower elevations. Snow depth ranges from 50 cm at low elevations to 100+ cm alpine. All layers should be monitored continuously.

Closing Summary

This report outlines current snowpack conditions characterized by a supportive melt-freeze crust with variable moisture and active storm and wind slab development in the alpine and treeline areas. While no significant avalanche observations have been reported, the combination of dry loose avalanches with evidence of pinwheeling and wet loose snowballing at treeline requires cautious travel. Immediate focus should be on avoiding high-risk alpine and ridgetop terrain, selecting moderate elevations with better snow bonding, and adjusting to weather changes. Prioritize safety through continual assessment and adherence to established risk management strategies.
