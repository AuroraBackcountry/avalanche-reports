Snowpack Structure

The surface consists of recent storm snow with buried surface hoar up to 10 mm at and below treeline. A thin melt-freeze crust is found near 80–145 cm depth, sometimes associated with the surface hoar interface. Near 230–250 cm lies an older, well-settled melt-freeze crust. Snow depth is greatest in the alpine (450+ cm), tapering quickly below 400–600 m. Buried surface hoar and crust layers form persistent weak layers requiring ongoing monitoring.

Key Actions & Safety Considerations

  • Monitor wind slab potential and evolving storm slabs overnight.
  • Avoid steep, trapped terrain near NW ranges.
  • Delay steep terrain exposure until further stabilization is confirmed.

Closing Summary

Focus on updated hazard awareness and cautious terrain selection. Prioritize slopes with firm, stable snow and avoid steep, exposed areas until conditions improve. Stay alert for changing weather and ongoing slab development.
